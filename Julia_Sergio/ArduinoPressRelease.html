May 24, 2017

Lilypad Arduino a Begineers Experience

Julia Dunbar and Sergio Ramirez Martin

News and Information

A Lilypad Arduino is an easy to use piece of hardware that
contains input sensors and output pins that allow the user
to play music, see lights, and interact with different sensors.
Lilypad Arduion's are a highly useful piece of technology that are
becoming more and more prominent within the health community
because of their uses for people of all ages to help with many
different health related needs.

This summer at Indiana University-Bloomington a small
group of REU students funded by the National Science Foundation
(NSF) performed a simple task with Lilypad
Arduino's. This task was aimed at teaching the students to work
with Lilypad Arduino's both software and hardware.

The ProHealth REU team of Sergio Ramirez Martin and Julia Dunbar
decided to implement the Harry Potter theme song provided by graduate
student Ben Jelen with their LilyPad Arduino. These REU students
complemented the song with RGB light values that are triggered depending
by the frequency detected by the light sensor. They also decided to add a
few led lights to further accompany the theme song and other features.

"I have never worked with a Lilypad Ardunio before and although it
took a little bit of a learning curve I enjoyed the outcome that we
received and I think they would be fun to work with in the future,"
said REU student Dunbar.

"I can't wait to see all that we will be able to do with the
Lilypad Arduino in the REU Program," said REU student Ramirez Martin.

For both students it's been an exciting first experience wokring with
a Lilypad Arduino. They were able to use their programming skills to help
them figure out the best way to accomplish their goal and also enjoyed
learning a new tool along the way. They hope to keep making progress and
coming with new and exciting ways to use Lilypad Arduino's to help those in need.
